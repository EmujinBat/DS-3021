# Emujin's case study

## Problem Focus Questions

### Q1. What problems are facing the organization?

A great amount of financial resources are wasted due to the U.S. education system not having educational software that meet the unique needs of individual school districts. 

### 2. What is the target variable and what type (reg or class) of solution are they developing?

Target variable - Mathematic software that meets the schools' needs. It is a classification. 

### 3. How would they know the solution was working (business metric)?

The non-profit gathered a post-implementation feedback from districts to measure the improved student performance, teacher satisfaction, and long-term cost savings. 

### 4. What other issues might the team be facing?

The non-profit team faced many issues relating to gathering a good quality data. Firstly, their data was relatively small and had response bias due to misinterpretation of the wording of the surveys. Secondly, they were concerned that some districts had unequal representation of certain characteristics. 

## Data to Concept Questions

### 1. How difficult is the data to gather?
Data collecting can be time-consuming and costly. The non-profit gathered data from school districts across the midwest. They asked questions such as teacher preparedness and administrative support which can be interpreted differently, leading to inconsistent data. I think ensuring the data collected is a quality data with minimal bias is the most challenging part. 

### 2. Is the target difficult to measure or break into smaller parts?
Yes. The target is difficult to measure because the school districts have different demographics which makes it hard to determine the effectiveness of the math softwares. It would need further careful considerations, accounting for each districts' context. 

### 3. What level of uncertainty (risk) are you willing to accept?
The non-profit seems to be willing to accept a moderate level of uncertainty because the potential impact of the recommender is great. While they try to minimize the uncertainty as much as possible, they still accept some response biases and overlooking certain details of some schools because it is simply impossible to avoid uncertainty 100% in any case.
